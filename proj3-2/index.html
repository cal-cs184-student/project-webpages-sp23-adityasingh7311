Part 4. Depth of Field

For these subparts, we recommend using a microfacet BSDF scene to show off the cool out of focus effects you can get with depth of field!

In a few sentences, explain the differences between a pinhole camera model and a thin-lens camera model.

In the idealistic pinhole model, all light passes through a point and is projected on a film. This makes it so that the entire image is in focus. In the thin-lens model, light is refracted through a lens. Only points at the focal distance are in focus which means parts of the simulated image can be in focus or blurred.
